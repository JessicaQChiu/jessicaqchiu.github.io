About Me

I'm a New Zealand business/IT graduate with professional experience working in the financial technology industry hoping to build a career in front-end development. I am open to developer opportunities in Vancouver or remote work in Canada. Being an operations analyst in a startup environment allowed me to build many transferrable skills such as learning new procedures, compliance with standards/practices, writing documentation, taking initiative with improvements, and more. Near the end of the NZ COVID-19 lockdown, I recognised the importance of being online - studying, working, connecting with others. I also recognised how a lot of technology isn't accessible - this inspired me to take the leap and pursue sustainable web development.

My first experiences with technology included playing games on a boxy PC. Neopets was a popular website during my childhood. When I discovered that I could customise my profile page - the cursor, colours, and add animations that would show for all my friends online, I was wonderstruck. Curiousity played a large part in my interest in tech and changing that single cursor design lead me down a path of developing interfaces for friends to use, programming a robotic arm, and volunteering to help spark that same enthusiasm in others.

I want to move forward by persistently developing my skills, diving into the knowledge of others, collaborating on cool projects, and providing my own perspective designing responsive, accessible web apps. People have always been at the center of my work and previous roles. I would love the opportunity to be a part of a team that helps create a positive impact on how information is used, communicated, and shared - to help create solutions or paths so anyone (regardless of what technology they use to browse the web) can find them. I hope to collaborate and bring my perspective of inclusion, accessibility, and sustainability when developing strategies and products.

It's difficult to summarise my sincerity to enter this industry and how I will be diligent in my contributions - but it is easy for me to say that I would love a chance to work together with you.
